Bamboo Ply

Durable in higher moisture content.
Takes little time to grow in comparision. 4 to 6 years. New shoots sprout right after cultivation. It's a type of grass. It holds record for fastes growing flora.
12% Harder than American maple, 25% harder than red oak. Where it shines is it's tensile strength which is comparable to steel(400MPa of structural steel, 200Mpa of Bamboo). You must have seen several story high architectural scaffolding made of bamboo.
Multi-layered, cross-laminated and heat-pressed can be 3 times more durable than traditional hardwood. Janka test (not scientific, but industry standard in woodwork industry) places it at between 3000 to 4000, depending upon quality of build.
No formaldehyde or any other harmful chemical is used during manufaturing ply. Due to high growth rate it sinks Carbon dioxide, it's carbon negative.

Points in favor

Europeans/Americans (uses most wood in houses) find it a costly option, since it is mostly imported from Asia. It grows here natively.
1927 Act classified it as tree, so harvest and transportation was not an option, it was illegal. 2017 Act deems it as a grass, so it can be transported and cultivated commercially.
